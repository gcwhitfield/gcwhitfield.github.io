"Slender" Remake

August 2019 - Game Programming/Design (Unity) & Music

Overview

I'm a huge fan of horror games. In middle school, I used to play a game called "Slender: The Eight Pages", where a tall slender man chases you around, moving only when you aren't looking at him. If you look at him for too long you die. I know - it sounds super corny. But at the time, it scared me to death!

This is actually something that I had wanted to work on for a long time. In fact, in high school I had spent many hours programing and designing a level for my own Slender game. I even made my own Slenderman model in blender. Sadly, I ended up trashing the project after I lost all of my work because I forgot to "Save Scene" and "Save Project" in Unity. Oops.

My second attempt at the game went much more smoothly. Instead of spending lots of time creating models and making everything from scratch, I focused more on programming and level design. I was shocked to discover how long it took to design (what I considered to be) a simple level in Unity. If I had to estimate, I would say that 60% of the time spent on this project was me placing objects and painting onto the terrain.

Although level design took a long time, the most difficult part of the project was the Slenderman follow script. It had to do several things:
  1. Move when the player is not looking
  2. Should not overlap with surrounding objects after moving to a new location
  3. Get closer to the player with each page collected
  4. Play death sound and animation when the player looks for too long
Since this was my first real Unity project (I had a ton of unfinished projects from before this), this script was a challenge. I didn't even know what a coroutine was. Needless to say, it was a really great learning experience.
