My game development history

I started my journey with video game programming way back when I was in middle school. I used to play Roblox religiously, and I especially enjoyed playing the obstacle course games, also called "obbies". I would even make my own obbies using Roblox Studio (Roblox's software tool for making worlds on their platform). Eventually, I started writing little Lua scripts to make my Roblox worlds more fun.

Fast forward a few years and I began high school. I didn't play Roblox anymore, but I was still interested in game development. I watched game development tutorials all the time and would mess around making little levels in Unity. For my first game dev development project popular I recreated the indie horror game called "Slender" in Unity. I had a few of my friends play it and I even scared some of my friends and family!

At CMU, I found other students at CMU with a similar passion for games as me when I joined Game Creation Society (GCS). It's a student organization where we create small games each semester. I'm currently in my spring semester of my senior year, and I have worked on 7 semester-long game projects with them so far. I've also worked on a few game jams here and there. Before CMU, I hadn't met anyone else that enjoyed making games as much as I do. It's been a pleasure working with GCS.

My most recent project with GCS was Lingua Litis, a 2 player word-spelling game where 2 wizards battle each other. I worked as a project lead and a programmer.

Also, I took a Computer Game Programming course last fall and I will be taking Advanced Game Studio this spring.
